About

Syllabus Decoder is a tool designed to help faculty and students understand AI policies in academic settings. We've developed a framework—using three dimensions (T-Tier for permissiveness, C-Level for compliance requirements, and E-Level for enforcement)—to translate complex policy language into clear, actionable categories. This dataset aggregates policies from 200+ courses across institutions, revealing patterns in how AI is governed in higher education. Our goal is to make policy frameworks more transparent and help educators design clear guidelines for the AI era.

T: Permissiveness Tier

How much AI usage is allowed

T0
Total prohibition
AI use is banned (or functionally banned) for the course or for all relevant work.
T1
Default prohibited, exceptions only
AI is not allowed unless explicitly permitted for a specific assignment or scenario.
T2
Permission-gated (ask first)
AI may be allowed, but students must get prior instructor approval (case-by-case).
T3
Bounded use allowed
AI allowed for limited support (brainstorming, outlining, clarity, proofreading), but not to "do the work."
T4
Allowed with transparency
AI is allowed to generate content, including on graded work, if it is properly disclosed and documented.
T5
Unrestricted or encouraged
Use of generative AI on any task is welcomed or mandatory, with constraints limited to responsibility and honesty.

C: Compliance

How much documentation is required

C0
None
No requirement to disclose or cite AI use.
C1
Minimal
Brief mention of AI use is required, but no specific citation format is mandated.
C2
Structured
Formal citation (APA/MLA) and specific attribution of AI's role is required.
C3
Full Audit
Requires submission of prompt logs, drafts, and comprehensive reflections on AI use.

E: Enforcement

How failure to comply is handled

E0
Trust-Based
Relies on student integrity; no explicit mention of monitoring or detection tools.
E1
Instructive
Warns about AI hallucinations and the general risks of academic dishonesty.
E2
Investigative
Explicitly mentions that AI detectors, manual audits, or version history will be used.
E3
Zero-Tolerance
Automatic referral to the student conduct office for any suspected violation.
